#  Harvard University 
##  Breakthroughs of 2025
Harvard scientists published thousands of studies in 2025, advancing knowledge and discovering solutions in a time when research funding is uncertain.
##  An extraordinary year
This year, the Harvard community has had research breakthroughs in genetics, artificial intelligence, disease treatments, and so much more.
* * *
###  Rewriting genetic destiny
David Liu received the 2025 Breakthrough Prize in Life Sciences for developing a revolutionary gene-editing platform that precisely corrects genetic mutations.
Learn more about David’s research 
[ Learn more about David’s research ](https://news.harvard.edu/gazette/story/2025/04/rewriting-genetic-destiny-gene-base-editing/)
###  Ancient DNA solves Uralic languages’ origins
Harvard researchers put Europe’s Uralic family of languages — which includes Hungarian, Finnish, and Estonian — a lot farther east than many thought.
Learn more about the researchers’ findings 
[ Learn more about the researchers’ findings ](https://news.harvard.edu/gazette/story/2025/07/ancient-dna-solves-mystery-of-hungarian-finnish-language-familys-origins/)
###  Speeding up rare disease diagnosis with AI
PopEVE, an AI model developed by the Debora Marks’ Lab, can identify the genetic variants most likely to cause severe disease and death.
Learn more about how the tool is being used 
[ Learn more about how the tool is being used ](https://hms.harvard.edu/news/new-artificial-intelligence-model-could-speed-rare-disease-diagnosis)
###  A quantum leap
Mikhail Lukin of the Quantum Science and Engineering Initiative, believes the core elements for building quantum computers are falling into place.
Learn more about the error detection and prevention system 
[ Learn more about the error detection and prevention system ](https://news.harvard.edu/gazette/story/2025/11/a-potential-quantum-leap/)
###  Three ways to treat drug-resistant tuberculosis
Carole Mitnick, the co-principal investigator, said the new approaches, which include recently discovered drugs, give new options for shorter, personalized treatment and are cleared for use for more people than ever.
Learn more about the medications 
[ Learn more about the medications ](https://hms.harvard.edu/news/study-finds-three-new-safe-effective-ways-treat-drug-resistant-tuberculosis)
###  Tracking climate change through nature’s “breaths”
Atmospheric chemist Steven Wofsy’s new research tower monitors Harvard Forest’s carbon intake and outtake, continuing data collection that he started in 1989.
Learn more about Steven’s ongoing research 
[ Learn more about Steven’s ongoing research ](https://news.harvard.edu/gazette/story/2025/10/tracking-climate-change-through-natures-breaths/)

##  The need for speed
Harvard’s School of Engineering researchers have developed a material that can dimple like a golf ball and change its aerodynamic properties on demand.
[ Learn more about this metamaterial  ](https://seas.harvard.edu/news/2025/10/need-speed)
[**Researching ways to design better with Simtables**](https://www.gsd.harvard.edu/2025/02/landscape-architecture-students-explore-pioneering-climate-visualization-techniques-to-inform-design/)
High-tech topography tables that utilize GIS data and computational modeling help architects understand and design for complex climate challenges.
[**Air conditioner use to surge as world gets hotter. Harvard startup has a solution.**](https://news.harvard.edu/gazette/story/2025/04/ac-use-to-surge-as-world-gets-hotter-harvard-startup-has-a-solution-climate-change/)
Harvard scientists have developed an energy-saving membrane, similar to a coffee filter, capable of separating water vapor directly from the air.
[**Physicists go to extremes to capture quantum materials** ](https://news.harvard.edu/gazette/story/2025/10/chilling-discovery/)
Rowland Institute researchers have pioneered a new way to achieve the coolest possible temperatures to image materials at a sub-atomic scale.

##  Pushing scholarship ahead
Throughout the humanities, breakthroughs and discoveries change the way we see the world and inform the way we improve it.
[ Learn more about how Harvard is innovating scholarship  ](https://news.harvard.edu/gazette/story/2025/05/hooking-first-years-on-the-arts-and-humanities/)

### Understanding the Earth
Through research and archeology, Harvard scientists got one step closer to [understanding the origins of life](https://news.harvard.edu/gazette/story/2025/07/a-step-toward-solving-central-mystery-of-life-on-earth/) and the early history of [Earth’s largest animal group](https://news.harvard.edu/gazette/story/newsplus/fossil-discovery-uncovers-early-history-of-earths-largest-animal-group/).
![An illustration showing a black hole within a cloud in space](https://www.harvard.edu/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/Han-Article-Banner-Image.jpg)
### Exploring the stars
Research papers from Harvard astronomers explored new evidence of the [closest known supermassive black hole](https://gsas.harvard.edu/news/massive-discovery) and a cosmic explosion that could be a rare flare from a magnetar—[one of the most mysterious objects in the universe](https://chandra.harvard.edu/press/25_releases/press_022525.html).
![Lauren Williams teaching](https://www.harvard.edu/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/macarthur_Math_1920.jpg)
### Crunching the numbers
Harvard’s [Lauren Williams won a MacArthur ‘genius grant’](https://news.harvard.edu/gazette/story/2025/10/lauren-williams-awarded-macarthur-genius-grant/) for her theoretical breakthroughs understanding phenomena such as tsunamis and traffic. 
![A plane flying over green land](https://www.harvard.edu/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/AdobeStock_460866760b.jpg)
### Protecting the planet
Harvard researchers are learning how to [capture emissions from fuels](https://news.harvard.edu/gazette/story/2025/09/seeking-a-carbon-capture-breakthrough/) and create [fuels from renewable sources](https://seas.harvard.edu/news/2025/11/next-frontier-clean-flight-jet-fuel-city-waste), like city waste.
